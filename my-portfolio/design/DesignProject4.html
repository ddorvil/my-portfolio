Introduction

The Curl Temp is a revolutionary product that that has ever been introduced to  be released to the beauty industry since it is never before seen. Curl Temp caters to people with curly or/ and kinky textured hair. The product can be used on anyone who has chemically treated their hair with bleach, dyes, or straightening creams, etc. While it will undoubtedly attract African Americans, any person with this texture can find use with this product. This product is supposed to, when used properly, give the user maximum moisture in their hair. The heat that is used is low enough not to burn the scalp but high enough to get the job done. The features that this product includes is the a diary log, data documentation on hair information/results, the slider to control the heat. One of the aspects that makes our product unique is that it is portable. A portable, moisturizing machine… now where have you seen that!

Data Objects:

Initial Survey on Hair Quality:

At the set-up stage of the product, the screen would obtain user’s information in a form of a questionnaire. Answers may be “YES/NO” , placement in a scale, and sentences.  From now on, functions would be recommended according to initial input.

Hair Diary- text:

Users may store diary entries about their hair conditions. The data includes humidity level, dryness discomfort level, softness of hair etc.

Level of Heat:

This is controlled by the slider.

Interaction Framework & Flow:

In this section, I will demonstrate the interaction flow in procedures:

Step 1:

Turn on the screen and the on/off button of console. There will be a parallel interaction pattern on screen, or a starter questionnaire about general hair health, if it is the first use.

(For First Use)

Upon first use, users would be asked to answer questions. According to the answers, the device would recommend hair care recipes, that is, level of moisturizing, heating, etc.

Step 2:

Put on the cap, and make sure the light on heat slider is on. Heat slider is a separate control to give more safety: user can adjust the heat on a more delicate level and shut it down directly.

Step 3:

Choose from the menu- the parallel interaction pattern enables multi-tasking. For example, user can select moisturizing and heating.

Step 4:

The conditioning process would start. On the menu, there will be time specifications from which user can adjust the time length of conditioning.

Step 5:

Once the conditioning was finished, the user can choose to put a hair care diary entry, using a keypad. Depending on user’s preference on privacy, the hair care service center may or may not access the information and make hair health analysis.

Reasonings related to scenario requirements:

Screen:

The screen informs the user of his/her hair conditions. This responds to a scenario requirement of “ability to track hair condition”.

The screen is in charge of menu control.

Interaction design pattern is parallel. 

This choice responds to the personas’ common need of saving time: users can apply multiple functions and achieve the desired effect faster.

Buttons on console:

 This responds to one of the scenario requirement of “ability to function fast”. Pushing the buttons can instantly execute a function.

Physical slider to control heat independently:

This design allows further exactness after the button control. This choice is coherent with the research focus group. Most interviewees were sensitive to the heat. Therefore, a slider could more delicately apply the heat.

Desired looks and sketches:

Design Language(s)

  • Convenient: Since Curl Temp should have an effect on the hair and it has to be hat-shape, it is preferably shown up like a Beanie, beaver hat or boater which can cover all of a person’s hair. A baseball cap size is impossible to cover and moisturize the hair. A Curl Temp could provide intuitive, innovative solutions. Related words such as brilliant and mature evokes sharp contrasts and a bit of sparkle. It implies a certain quiet orderliness and perhaps a bit of conservatism in visual style. It is easy and nice looking enough for users to wear while either outside or in the comfort of their homes. The color of the Curl Temp could be mixed with black and white on top and light pink at the edge for ladies and light grey for gentlemen.
  • Efficient: Customers expect the Curl Temp to be more like furniture than computers: long-lasting and reliable. Some do not want to heat their hair and would be impatient with a Curl Temp that crashed or made them look stupid. This implies a sense of solidity and quality in fit and finish, along with straightforward visual communication. Related words include professional and competent.
  • High-end: A Curl Temp is designed for people who can afford to spend a lot for caring for their hair. A high-end product will not demand user to input too much information. Instead, it can distribute different moisturizing strategies to different users. It needs to be the sort of thing an executive would want to show off and anyone would want to use—professional, but an object of desire all the same. Related words include unique, elite, advanced, sleek, and inspired. Although the Curl Temp will rely heavily on an interface and the moisture, and hardware, it still has to look familiar with a known platform(some head gear like a bonnet), simple and beautiful.

Brief Summary

Based on the advantages and disadvantages listed above, we thoroughly think that the Curl Temp should get rid of the property of stylus on a separate pad. Keep track on the initial requirements and big ideas, we want the product to be portable, customizable, and high-end. However, the stylus might make it easier to lose and slower to function. Since Curl Temp aims to be the best, quickest product in the market, this property should not exist. Instead, the touch screen would be the most important part to look into.
