ABOUT

Pratt Institute, a renowned design school, recently revamped their website to modernize and improve user experience. I along with a team of 4 analyzed user engagement on key pages (News, Events, Daily Hub, Prattfolio) using Google Analytics 4. I focused on the Daily Hub while my team members focused on the other pages. To read the full report click here. The Daily Hub is essentially the current events of all things Pratt Institute. It captures quick snapshots of all accolades of alumni and current students from solo exhibitions, awards, publications, and more!

RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

How might we increase engagement and time spent on the Daily Hub?

How motivate users to explore Pratt's academic offerings?

How might we improve conversion rates from Daily Hub visitors to program inquiries.

RESEARCH & ANALYSIS

Google Analytics 4: Analyzed user behavior, page views, and engagement metrics.

Path Exploration: Identified navigation patterns and user journeys.

Benchmarking: Studied similar pages at other design schools.

FINDINGS

Low engagement: Average time spent on the Daily Hub was only 36 seconds.

Disconnect from programs: Users weren't clicking through to program pages.

Lack of clear CTAs: The "Apply" button's context was unclear.

Outdated content: Pages with older achievements received fewer views.

RECOMMENDATIONS

Recommendation 1

Headline titles: Improve scannability and attract users to relevant achievements.

Visual components: Add pictures and gifs to increase engagement.

Focus on people and achievements: Highlight individuals, majors, and achievement types.

Filter and sort functionality: Allow users to personalize their experience.

Archive older content: Keep the page focused on current achievements.

Recommendation 2

Embed articles from Pratt websites: Keep users within Pratt's domain.

Add clear and contextually relevant CTAs: Guide users towards program pages.

"Take the Next Step" banner: Encourage further exploration of Pratt's offerings.

MEASURING SUCCESS

Increased time spent on the Daily Hub.

Higher click-through rates to program pages.

Improved conversion rates from Daily Hub visitors to program inquiries.

Positive user feedback on the redesigned page through surveys or interviews

CLIENT FEEDBACK

  • They loved the examples from peer institutions
  • Helped encourage their brainstorming on how to have more CTAs for lesser viewed pages like the Daily Hub
  • They found the Daily Hub recommendations really helpful such as internal links and connecting others pages from Pratt on the Daily Hub
  • Overall, they liked that the recommendations for the Daily Hub can be used for other pages as well!

    • CONCLUSION

    By implementing these recommendations, Pratt can transform the Daily Hub into a powerful tool for inspiring and converting prospective students. This case study demonstrates a data-driven approach to UX research and design, with clear goals, actionable insights, and measurable outcomes.
